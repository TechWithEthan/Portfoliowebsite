How to Create and Share a Google Form in Google Meet

Creating and sharing a Google Form during a Google Meet session can enhance interaction, gather feedback, and conduct surveys or quizzes seamlessly. Here’s a professional guide to help you create and share a Google Form during your next Google Meet.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Create the Google Form

  1. Open Google Forms: Go to Google Forms.
  2. Start a New Form: Click on the blank form or choose a template that suits your needs.
  3. Title and Description: Add a title and description to your form to give participants context.
  4. Add Questions: Use the "plus" icon to add questions. You can choose from multiple question types like multiple choice, checkboxes, short answer, etc.
  5. Customize the Form: Use the palette icon to change the theme, add images, or videos to make the form more engaging.
  6. Settings: Click on the settings icon (gear) to configure options like collecting email addresses, allowing edit responses, and showing a progress bar.

2. Get the Google Form Link

  1. Click on Send: Once your form is ready, click on the “Send” button at the top right corner.
  2. Get Link: In the Send form dialog, click on the link icon (chain link).
  3. Shorten URL: Optionally, you can check the “Shorten URL” box to get a simpler link.
  4. Copy the Link: Click on “Copy” to save the link to your clipboard.

3. Join or Start a Google Meet

  1. Open Google Meet: Go to Google Meet.
  2. Start a Meeting: Click on “New meeting” to start a new session or join an existing meeting.
  3. Invite Participants: Share the meeting link with participants or add them using their email addresses.

4. Share the Google Form in Google Meet

  1. Share via Chat: During the meeting, click on the chat icon at the bottom right corner.
  2. Paste the Link: Paste the copied Google Form link into the chat box and press Enter. This allows all participants to access the form instantly.
  3. Announce the Form: Inform participants about the form and encourage them to fill it out. You can explain the purpose and how it will be used.

5. Monitor Responses

  1. Access Responses: Return to Google Forms and click on the “Responses” tab.
  2. View Responses: You can view individual responses, see summary charts, and export data to Google Sheets for further analysis.
  3. Share Results: If needed, you can share the results with participants by generating a summary link or sharing the Google Sheet.

Tips for a Professional Experience

  • Preparation: Prepare your Google Form in advance to ensure it aligns with your meeting agenda.
  • Engagement: Use interactive question types and multimedia to make the form engaging.
  • Clarity: Clearly explain the purpose of the form and how the data will be used to ensure transparency.
  • Follow-up: Share the results or insights gathered from the form with participants as a follow-up, demonstrating that their input is valued.

By following these steps, you can create a seamless and interactive experience for your audience during your Google Meet sessions. This approach not only enhances engagement but also provides valuable insights that can be used to improve future meetings or projects.
